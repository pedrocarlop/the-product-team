# Role Catalog

This is the canonical specialist-role catalog for orchestrator staffing decisions.

- Read the full catalog before any best-team assessment or staffing decision.
- Review every business, design, and engineering role before narrowing the team.
- `orchestrator` and `reference` are intentionally excluded because they are workflow support roles, not staffed specialists.
- Generated from `agents/*/*/*.toml`. Refresh with `python3 scripts/render_role_catalog.py --write`.

## Business Roles

- `business-analyst` (Business Analyst, executor) — Why: Bridges business problems and technical solutions by eliciting, documenting, and validating requirements that teams can act on. Owns: process mapping, stakeholder requirements elicitation, and gap analysis.
- `business-development-director` (Business Development Director, executor) — Why: Originates and structures strategic deals, enters new markets, and builds the external relationships that create non-linear growth opportunities. Owns: strategic deal origination, market entry analysis, and partnership strategy.
- `chief-product-officer` (Chief Product Officer, executor) — Why: Sets the product vision and strategy, allocates the product portfolio, and leads the organization that builds the company's future. Owns: product vision, portfolio strategy, and org-level product direction.
- `customer-success-manager` (Customer Success Manager, executor) — Why: Drives customer retention, expansion, and measurable business outcomes by owning the post-sale relationship from onboarding through renewal and growth. Owns: onboarding strategy, retention analysis, and expansion and renewal.
- `data-analyst` (Data Analyst, executor) — Why: Transforms raw data into decisions by framing the right question, running rigorous analysis, and communicating findings in terms of business implications. Owns: metrics analysis, dashboard design, and A/B test analysis.
- `financial-analyst` (Financial Analyst, executor) — Why: Builds financial models, interprets business performance, and turns numbers into decisions for a SaaS company. Owns: financial modeling, SaaS metrics, and unit economics.
- `growth-manager` (Growth Manager, executor) — Why: Drives measurable, compounding growth across the full acquisition-to-revenue funnel by running disciplined experiments, building growth models, and removing bottlenecks at every stage. Owns: acquisition funnel analysis, conversion optimization, and retention strategy.
- `marketing-manager` (Marketing Manager, executor) — Why: Builds and executes the marketing strategy that generates qualified demand, establishes category positioning, and enables revenue growth through owned, earned, and paid channels. Owns: marketing strategy, demand generation, and brand management.
- `operations-manager` (Operations Manager, executor) — Why: Designs and improves the operational systems, processes, and coordination mechanisms that make the business run predictably, efficiently, and at scale. Owns: operational systems design, process optimization, and coordination mechanisms.
- `partnership-manager` (Partnership Manager, executor) — Why: Identifies, structures, and activates partnerships that create measurable distribution, revenue, or product leverage. Owns: partner identification, deal structure design, and partner activation.
- `product-manager` (Product Manager, executor) — Why: Drives product discovery, prioritization, and delivery coordination to ship the right thing at the right time. Owns: product discovery, prioritization, and delivery coordination.
- `product-marketing-manager` (Product Marketing Manager, executor) — Why: Defines positioning, equips sales with the narrative and tools to win, and ensures every product launch converts to measurable adoption. Owns: positioning, GTM strategy, and launch planning.
- `requirements-author` (Requirements Author, executor) — Why: Turn orchestrator intake into a design-ready, implementation-aware requirements package. Owns: PRD authoring, REQ-* and AC-* definition, and scope, risks, assumptions, and edge-case framing.
- `requirements-reviewer` (Requirements Reviewer, reviewer) — Why: Review the PRD for clarity, completeness, and readiness for design and engineering. Owns: PRD quality review, requirements gate recommendation, and consolidated remediation for requirements defects.
- `revenue-operations` (Revenue Operations, executor) — Why: Designs and operates the systems, processes, and data infrastructure that make revenue generation predictable, measurable, and scalable across sales, marketing, and customer success. Owns: revenue systems design, CRM configuration, and pipeline operations.
- `strategy-lead` (Strategy Lead, executor) — Why: Frames the company's most important strategic questions, synthesizes data and market intelligence into decisions, and drives the planning processes that align the organization. Owns: strategic framing, market intelligence synthesis, and planning processes.

## Design Roles

- `accessibility-designer` (Accessibility Designer, executor) — Why: Ensure every user can access, navigate, and succeed with the product — auditing designs and implementations against WCAG standards, catching exclusions before they ship, and building accessibility into the system rather than retrofitting it afterward. Owns: WCAG compliance audit, keyboard navigation spec, and screen reader support spec.
- `content-designer` (Content Designer, executor) — Why: Write the words inside the product — microcopy, CTAs, error messages, empty states, onboarding, and conversational UX — with a consistent voice, measurable clarity, and zero ambiguity. Owns: microcopy, CTAs, and error messages.
- `conversation-designer` (Conversation Designer, executor) — Why: Design the language, logic, and failure recovery of AI-powered conversations and dialogue flows. Owns: dialogue flow design, prompt and response patterns, and conversational error recovery.
- `copy-reviewer` (Copy Reviewer, reviewer) — Why: Validate clarity, brevity, consistency, and actionability of interface copy. Owns: microcopy review, terminology consistency review, and message clarity and actionability review.
- `design-director` (Design Director, executor) — Why: Set the design vision, raise the quality bar, build the team, and represent design as a strategic function at the organizational level — so that design is a competitive advantage, not a delivery service. Owns: design vision, quality bar setting, and design team building.
- `design-ops` (Design Ops, executor) — Why: Build and run the operational infrastructure that makes the design organization effective at scale — processes, tooling, quality systems, resource coordination, and the organizational glue that keeps design from becoming a bottleneck. Owns: Figma governance, design handoff infrastructure, and design tooling.
- `design-system-reviewer` (Design System Reviewer, reviewer) — Why: Validate component usage, composition quality, and design-system alignment. Owns: design-system compliance review, pattern drift detection, and custom-component exception review.
- `design-systems-designer` (Design Systems Designer, executor) — Why: Build and govern the component architecture, token system, and usage guidelines that make the design system the team's single source of truth — for both design and code. Owns: token architecture, component library specs, and design-system contribution guidelines.
- `design-technologist` (Design Technologist, executor) — Why: Close the gap between design intent and engineering reality — implementing design system tokens in code, building high-fidelity prototypes, running design QA, and serving as the technical translator between design and engineering. Owns: prototype code, token implementation, and Storybook stories.
- `information-architect` (Information Architect, executor) — Why: Design the structural skeleton — navigation, taxonomy, content groupings, labeling systems, and findability at scale. Owns: navigation structure, taxonomy and labeling systems, and content groupings and findability.
- `localization-designer` (Localization Designer, executor) — Why: Design product experiences that work across target locales including RTL support, text expansion, and cultural adaptation. Owns: multi-locale layout adaptation, RTL support design, and text expansion handling.
- `motion-designer` (Motion Designer, executor) — Why: Design motion that communicates — transitions, microinteractions, loading states, and gesture animations — with precise timing, easing, and performance characteristics that feel intentional, not decorative. Owns: animation specs, transition definitions, and loading state motion.
- `product-designer` (Product Designer, executor) — Why: Translate approved requirements into a repo-aware and source-aware UX and UI package. Owns: task flows, screen and state design, and component mapping.
- `service-designer` (Service Designer, executor) — Why: Map and design the full end-to-end service experience including backstage processes and cross-channel touchpoints. Owns: service blueprints, cross-channel experience mapping, and backstage process design.
- `ui-designer` (UI Designer, executor) — Why: Design the precise visual surface with full design-system fidelity and complete state coverage. Owns: visual surface design, component state coverage, and typography and spacing system.
- `usability-reviewer` (Usability Reviewer, reviewer) — Why: Review the design package against usability heuristics and accessibility-adjacent interaction risks. Owns: heuristic usability review, discoverability and feedback review, and cognitive-load and reversibility review.
- `ux-designer` (UX Designer, executor) — Why: Map user intent to product structure through flows, wireframes, IA, and usability validation. Owns: user task flows, wireframes and interaction scaffolding, and information architecture within the feature scope.
- `ux-flow-reviewer` (UX Flow Reviewer, reviewer) — Why: Validate sequence logic, task completion, and recovery paths across the UX flows. Owns: flow logic review, transition and recovery-path review, and dead-end and friction detection.
- `ux-researcher` (UX Researcher, executor) — Why: Produce research evidence — user interviews, usability testing, synthesis, and insight generation — before design decisions are made. Owns: research planning and synthesis, user interview analysis, and usability test design.

## Engineering Roles

- `api-designer` (Api Designer, executor) — Why: Designs API contracts that are consistent, evolvable, and immediately usable by consumers without requiring out-of-band explanation. Owns: API contract design, OpenAPI specifications, and versioning strategy.
- `backend-engineer` (Backend Engineer, executor) — Why: Designs and implements reliable, secure, and observable server-side systems — from API contracts and business logic to data persistence and production operations. Owns: API contract implementation, business logic, and data persistence.
- `data-engineer` (Data Engineer, executor) — Why: Designs, builds, and operates reliable data pipelines and warehouses that deliver trusted, well-documented data to analysts and product teams. Owns: data pipeline design, ETL workflows, and warehouse schema.
- `database-engineer` (Database Engineer, executor) — Why: Designs, optimizes, and maintains database systems with a focus on correctness, performance, and operational safety. Owns: schema design, migration authoring, and query optimization.
- `devops-engineer` (Devops Engineer, executor) — Why: Designs, builds, and operates the infrastructure and delivery pipelines that move code safely from commit to production at high reliability. Owns: CI/CD pipeline design, infrastructure configuration, and deployment automation.
- `engineer` (Engineer, executor) — Why: Implement the approved experience using the target repository's existing conventions and any named source-system repository. Owns: technical implementation plan, code changes, and implementation notes and traceability to approved design.
- `engineering-manager` (Engineering Manager, executor) — Why: Manages engineering team health, delivery, and growth while representing engineering constraints across the organization. Owns: team health assessment, delivery coordination, and engineering capacity planning.
- `engineering-reviewer` (Engineering Reviewer, reviewer) — Why: Validate implemented work against the approved package and production-readiness expectations. Owns: implementation parity review, production-risk review, and final engineering gate recommendation.
- `frontend-engineer` (Frontend Engineer, executor) — Why: Translates design specs and product requirements into accessible, performant, and maintainable UI components that ship with confidence. Owns: UI component architecture, client-side state management, and design-to-code translation.
- `fullstack-engineer` (Fullstack Engineer, executor) — Why: Owns complete features end-to-end — from database schema through API contract to rendered UI — without handing off between disciplines or losing fidelity across layer boundaries. Owns: end-to-end feature implementation from schema to rendered UI, API and client integration, and full-stack state management.
- `ml-engineer` (Ml Engineer, executor) — Why: Frames, builds, evaluates, and operates machine learning and LLM-powered systems from experiment to production. Owns: LLM integration, model serving, and embeddings and RAG pipelines.
- `mobile-engineer` (Mobile Engineer, executor) — Why: Builds, tests, and ships high-quality iOS and Android experiences using React Native and Expo. Owns: iOS and Android implementation, React Native or native platform code, and mobile-specific state and navigation.
- `performance-engineer` (Performance Engineer, executor) — Why: Measures, diagnoses, and improves web and backend performance to meet defined user-experience budgets and reliability SLAs. Owns: Core Web Vitals optimization, load testing, and latency budget enforcement.
- `qa-engineer` (Qa Engineer, executor) — Why: Owns test strategy, test implementation, and quality gates that give the team confidence to ship without regressions. Owns: test strategy, E2E test suites, and regression test suites.
- `sales-engineer` (Sales Engineer, executor) — Why: Bridges technical product depth and sales execution to help prospects evaluate and adopt the product with confidence. Owns: technical pre-sales, demo environment design, and POC builds.
- `security-engineer` (Security Engineer, executor) — Why: Identifies, prevents, and remediates security vulnerabilities across the application, infrastructure, and development pipeline before they reach production or become incidents. Owns: auth flow implementation, OWASP compliance, and secrets management.
- `solutions-architect` (Solutions Architect, executor) — Why: Translates business requirements into end-to-end technical architectures that are secure, scalable, cost-governed, and buildable by the teams who will own them. Owns: system design across services, vendor evaluation, and scalability planning.
- `tech-lead` (Tech Lead, executor) — Why: Sets technical direction, maintains architectural integrity, and grows engineering capability across a team or product area. Owns: architectural decisions, cross-team technical impact assessment, and technical standards.
