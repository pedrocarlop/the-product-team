# Qualify

## Purpose

Use this skill to decide whether a potential partner is worth investing time and resources in, and to state why in writing.

## When to Use

- When a sourced list needs prioritization
- When leadership wants a go/no-go recommendation before outreach or scoping
- When the opportunity needs a clearer read on fit, leverage, and risk

## When Not to Use

- When the main problem is discovering new targets
- When the commercial structure is already the focus
- When the work is about onboarding or operating an active partner

## Required Inputs

- The sourced target list or specific partnership opportunity
- The business goal the partnership is meant to support
- Any known internal constraints such as product, legal, finance, or engineering capacity
- The partner's likely audience, channel power, integration value, or deal reach
- Any evidence already collected, even if incomplete

## Workflow

1. State the partnership opportunity in one sentence, including the value being pursued.
2. Test whether the partner has real leverage: audience, distribution, product adjacency, or market access.
3. Check mutual value by asking what the partner gets in return and whether it is compelling enough.
4. Review feasibility, timing, and internal constraints that could block execution.
5. Surface the main risks, the earliest disconfirming signal, and the decision deadline.
6. Write a clear recommendation to pursue, pause, or drop the opportunity.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Clear business logic over enthusiasm
- Specificity over market-size theater
- Evidence-backed assumptions over vague optimism
- Decision usefulness over narrative polish
- Alignment with company capabilities and timing

## Output Contract

- A concise qualification verdict for each opportunity
- The supporting assumptions and evidence gaps
- The key risks or disconfirming signals to watch for
- A clear recommendation on next action

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Opportunity: A regional software vendor wants a referral partnership
- Goal: Open a new distribution channel

Expected output:
- Verdict: Qualified if the vendor can influence purchase decisions at scale and has a clear reason to prioritize us
- Rationale: States why the opportunity matters and what must be true for it to create value

## Guardrails

- Do not confuse a large partner with a good partner
- Do not qualify opportunities that rely on unconfirmed future work
- Do not approve work without a believable path to execution
- Do not skip the failure case or disconfirming signal
