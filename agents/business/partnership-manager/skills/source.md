# Source

## Purpose

Use this skill to build a qualified list of partnership targets with enough context on strategic fit, integration potential, and relationship access to prioritize outreach.

## When to Use

- When starting a partnership motion and needing candidate organizations to evaluate
- When mapping an ecosystem to understand who plays what role and where partnership leverage exists
- When identifying champions, integration owners, and relationship entry points within target organizations

## When Not to Use

- When the target list already exists and the work is to negotiate terms or structure a deal
- When the issue is partnership operations, onboarding, or ongoing health management
- When the task is competitive intelligence without a partnership intent

## Required Inputs

- The partnership goal: channel, technology integration, co-sell, distribution, or strategic alliance
- Filters: geography, company size, industry vertical, integration type, or platform compatibility
- Must-haves, exclusions, and ideal partner profile characteristics
- The desired output format: target list, ecosystem map, stakeholder map, or intelligence brief
- Any existing partner relationships that constrain or inform the search

## Workflow

1. Clarify the partnership type and the specific business outcome the partnership must enable.
2. Map the relevant ecosystem: who are the key players, what roles do they fill, and where are the gaps.
3. Build a candidate list with integration fit, market reach, and partnership motivation for each target.
4. Identify decision-makers, partnership team contacts, and internal champions where available.
5. Rank targets by strategic alignment, integration readiness, relationship access, and execution likelihood.
6. Remove weak leads, flag timing constraints, and surface the strongest paths to a first partnership conversation.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Integration and ecosystem fit over generic company size
- Strategic alignment with the partnership goal over broad market presence
- Relationship access quality over mere contact availability
- Evidence of partnership motivation (public signals, existing integrations) over assumption
- Actionable next steps over exhaustive research volume

## Output Contract

- A ranked list of partnership targets with strategic fit rationale for each
- Ecosystem map or positioning notes showing where each target fits in the landscape
- Known stakeholders, partnership contacts, and relationship entry points
- Integration readiness or compatibility notes where relevant
- Gaps that need further research or internal validation

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Goal: Find technology integration partners for a new API platform launch
- Filters: B2B SaaS companies in the project management or CRM categories with existing marketplace programs

Expected output:
- A ranked list of 8-12 companies with notes on integration fit, marketplace maturity, and likely partnership team contacts
- Ecosystem map showing how the targets relate to each other and to the platform
- Priority recommendation: which 3 targets to approach first and why

## Guardrails

- Do not treat a long, unqualified list as a finished sourcing deliverable
- Do not over-index on company size when integration fit and partnership motivation matter more
- Do not confuse having a contact name with having a real relationship path
- Do not ignore timing signals such as recent funding rounds, leadership changes, or platform announcements
- Do not source without understanding the partnership type and business outcome first

## Optional Tools / Resources

- Web search for company, market, and competitive research
- CRM or pipeline records for existing relationship history
- Partner directories, marketplace listings, and integration catalogs
- Internal notes on prior outreach, partnerships, and deal history
