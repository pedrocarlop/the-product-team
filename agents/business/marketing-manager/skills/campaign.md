# Campaign

## Purpose

Use this skill to design a campaign that can actually ship: a clear objective, audience, offer, message, channel mix, timing, budget, and measurement plan.

## When to Use

- When launching a new product, feature, event, promotion, or content theme
- When multiple assets or teams need to be coordinated
- When the campaign needs a written brief before work begins
- When budget or timing decisions need to be locked before execution

## When Not to Use

- When the main task is choosing the market position or message
- When the work is primarily ongoing channel optimization
- When the campaign already exists and the task is only measuring or reporting it

## Required Inputs

- Campaign objective and business outcome
- Target audience and buyer journey stage
- Offer, CTA, or conversion event
- Available channels, budget, and timing constraints
- Tracking requirements, owners, and approval process

## Workflow

1. Write the campaign objective in one sentence and tie it to a measurable business outcome.
2. Define the audience, journey stage, and key message for the campaign.
3. Choose the smallest channel mix that can plausibly reach the objective.
4. List required assets, owners, dependencies, and approvals.
5. Define tracking requirements before launch, including UTMs and conversion events.
6. Set success metrics, review cadence, and a clear stop or iterate decision point.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Campaigns should be scoped to a real objective, not activity for its own sake
- Every execution choice should support the audience and offer
- Tracking should be designed before launch, not patched afterward
- Ownership and dependencies should be explicit
- The plan should be understandable by stakeholders who are not in marketing

## Output Contract

- Campaign brief with objective, audience, message, channels, budget, and timing
- Asset checklist with owners and dependencies
- Tracking plan with required UTMs and conversion events
- Review or launch readiness notes

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Goal: Increase qualified trial signups for a new feature launch
- Constraints: Small budget, two weeks to launch

Expected output:
- Campaign brief with a single objective tied to trial signups
- Channel mix limited to the highest-intent channels
- Asset and tracking checklist covering landing page, email, paid creative, and UTMs
- Launch readiness note that blocks spend until tracking is verified

## Guardrails

- Do not launch without a measurable objective
- Do not assume the channel mix before the audience is clear
- Do not leave tracking, ownership, or approvals ambiguous
- Do not create campaign complexity that is larger than the goal
