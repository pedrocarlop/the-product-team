# Onboard

## Purpose

Use this skill to turn a signed customer into an activated customer by defining the first-value path, the success plan, and the operating cadence that gets the account moving.

## When to Use

- When a new account needs a kickoff plan
- When onboarding is stuck, vague, or not tied to value
- When a customer segment needs a repeatable onboarding playbook

## When Not to Use

- When the main problem is a deteriorating account relationship or churn risk
- When you are forecasting renewal or planning expansion
- When the customer is already active and only health monitoring is needed

## Required Inputs

- Customer name, segment, and ARR tier
- Customer business outcome and first-value definition
- Product scope, implementation constraints, and stakeholders
- Timeline, contract close date, and any promised milestones
- Known blockers, dependencies, and internal owners

## Workflow

1. Define the customer's desired business outcome and the first-value moment.
2. Break the path to first value into a small set of activation milestones.
3. Assign an owner, target date, and proof of completion to each milestone.
4. Build a 30/60/90-day plan that reflects the customer's actual adoption path.
5. Confirm kickoff cadence, meeting owners, and escalation path for blockers.
6. Verify the plan is realistic against implementation dependencies and customer capacity.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- First value before feature completeness
- Milestones must be observable and time-bound
- The plan should reflect the customer's business outcome, not internal setup steps
- Ownership should be explicit for every milestone
- Onboarding should create momentum, not just documentation

## Output Contract

- A concise onboarding plan with milestones, owners, and dates
- A kickoff agenda or customer-facing summary when needed
- Notes on risks, dependencies, or missing stakeholders

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Customer: Mid-market sales team
- Outcome: Reduce rep ramp time by 20%
- Constraint: Limited implementation bandwidth

Expected output:
- A 30/60/90-day plan with a first-value milestone tied to first active workflow completion
- A kickoff agenda that prioritizes the smallest path to measurable adoption

## Guardrails

- Do not confuse implementation work with customer success
- Do not set milestones you cannot verify
- Do not overload the customer with too many concurrent actions
- Do not skip stakeholder alignment for the economic buyer or champion
