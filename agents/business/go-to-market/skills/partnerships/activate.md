# Activate

## Purpose

Use this skill to convert a signed partnership into a working motion that produces pipeline, revenue, or product leverage.

## When to Use

- When an agreement is signed and the partnership needs to go live
- When the partner must be onboarded, trained, or equipped
- When the partnership needs a recurring operating cadence and health review

## When Not to Use

- When the partnership is still being sourced or qualified
- When the structure and terms are not yet agreed
- When the work is only about researching possible targets

## Required Inputs

- The signed agreement or explicit launch approval
- The launch scope and success metrics
- Named owners on both sides
- Any product, legal, marketing, or engineering dependencies
- The partner toolkit, enablement assets, and launch timeline

## Workflow

1. Confirm the partnership is actually ready to launch, including any required approvals or dependencies.
2. Assign owners, communication paths, and a launch date on both sides.
3. Deliver the enablement package, kickoff agenda, and operational runbook.
4. Set initial 30/60/90-day activity targets and reporting expectations.
5. Establish the health score, review cadence, and escalation path.
6. Document the first operating review and next actions so the motion does not stall.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Execution clarity over ceremonial launch activity
- Partner readiness over internal assumptions
- Measurable milestones over vague enthusiasm
- Consistent follow-through over one-time kickoff energy
- Early visibility into risk over late surprise

## Output Contract

- A launch plan with owners, dates, and milestones
- The partner enablement and onboarding package
- The tracking and health cadence
- Any blocked dependencies or follow-up actions

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Partnership: Signed referral agreement with a new channel partner

Expected output:
- Launch plan with kickoff date, partner training, first referral targets, and monthly health review
- Notes on any materials or approvals still needed

## Guardrails

- Do not activate a partnership without a signed agreement or explicit launch approval
- Do not assume the partner will self-activate without training and follow-up
- Do not skip owner assignment or reporting setup
- Do not treat kickoff as the end of the work
