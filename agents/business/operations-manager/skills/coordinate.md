# Coordinate

## Purpose

Use this skill to keep cross-functional work moving by making ownership, timing, information transfer, and escalation paths explicit.

## When to Use

- When a task crosses team boundaries and risks getting stuck
- When multiple owners need a shared cadence or decision path
- When a recurring meeting or update channel needs to be designed or cleaned up

## When Not to Use

- When the main issue is still understanding the process itself
- When the work should be standardized into a repeatable SOP
- When the goal is to fix a recurring operational failure at the root cause

## Required Inputs

- The shared objective or deliverable
- The teams, owners, and decision makers involved
- Known dependencies, deadlines, and blockers
- The communication channels or meetings already in use
- Any escalation rules, SLAs, or compliance constraints

## Workflow

1. Identify every owner, dependency, and decision required to move the work forward.
2. Map the handoff points and the exact information each receiver needs.
3. Define the cadence, channel, and format for updates or decisions.
4. Set escalation rules for stalled decisions, missed SLAs, or missing inputs.
5. Remove unnecessary meetings or status loops that do not change outcomes.
6. Confirm the coordination plan is simple enough that people will actually follow it.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Make ownership visible and unambiguous
- Prefer the smallest coordination mechanism that still works
- Decisions should happen where the best context lives
- Escalation should be pre-agreed, not improvised
- Coordination should reduce friction, not create a new reporting burden

## Output Contract

- A coordination plan or handoff protocol
- A list of owners, dependencies, and decision points
- A meeting or communication cadence, if needed
- An escalation path for misses or blockers

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Situation: "Marketing, finance, and operations all need to approve a launch checklist."

Expected output:
- Coordination note: "Marketing owns readiness updates, finance owns budget signoff, and operations owns launch go/no-go. Each team sends status in the same template 48 hours before launch, with escalation to the role lead if a decision is late."

## Guardrails

- Do not create coordination overhead that is heavier than the work itself
- Do not leave dependencies without an owner or date
- Do not use meetings as a substitute for decision making
- Do not assume people will infer the right handoff without explicit instructions
