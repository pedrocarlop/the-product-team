# Launch

## Purpose

Use this skill to turn a product release into a coordinated market event with clear owners, deadlines, and measurable outcomes.

## When to Use

- When a feature, release, or market initiative needs a launch plan
- When stakeholders need tiering, sequencing, or cross-functional coordination
- When launch success needs to be defined before the announcement goes out

## When Not to Use

- When the work is only about naming, messaging, or segmentation
- When the release is too minor for coordinated launch treatment
- When the task is primarily post-launch adoption diagnosis or enablement content

## Required Inputs

- What is being launched and why it matters
- Launch audience, channels, and timing constraints
- Cross-functional dependencies and owners
- The expected business outcome and how it will be measured
- Any product, legal, or support constraints that affect timing or claims

## Workflow

1. Classify the launch scope and decide the launch tier.
2. Define the audience, channel mix, and release timing.
3. Build a checklist with owners for product, marketing, sales, support, and any other involved teams.
4. Establish the success metric, baseline, and measurement window before launch day.
5. Write the launch narrative and operational plan so every team knows what to do.
6. Review readiness, dependencies, and follow-up actions after launch.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Launches should be planned, not improvised
- Success metrics should be defined before publication
- Every external message should have an internal owner
- The plan should be simple enough for teams to execute under deadline pressure

## Output Contract

- Launch tier and rationale
- Launch plan with owners, milestones, and deadlines
- Success metrics and measurement plan
- Launch communications or checklist notes, if requested

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Release: New enterprise reporting dashboard
- Need: Coordinate launch across product, marketing, and sales

Expected output:
- Launch tier: Tier 1
- Launch plan: Cross-functional checklist, announcement timing, sales readiness, support prep, and 90-day adoption measurement

## Guardrails

- Do not treat publication as the end of the launch
- Do not define success only in terms of internal excitement
- Do not skip readiness checks because the release is "just a feature"
- Do not create a launch plan without owners
