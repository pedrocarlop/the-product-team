# Differentiate

## Purpose

Use this skill to translate competitive intelligence into a credible reason to choose this product over the next-best alternative.

## When to Use

- When the task involves competitor comparisons or sales objections
- When battlecards or competitor-specific talk tracks need updating
- When win/loss patterns suggest the market sees us differently than we see ourselves

## When Not to Use

- When the task is only about defining the core market position
- When the request is launch planning or general enablement
- When the comparison point is vague enough that no real alternative is being evaluated

## Required Inputs

- The competitor or alternative being compared
- The buying scenario where the comparison matters
- Current win/loss signals, call notes, or customer feedback
- Factual proof points that support the differentiating claim
- Known areas where the competitor legitimately wins

## Workflow

1. Identify the real buying scenario and the alternative buyers are considering.
2. Separate your own story from the competitor's actual story.
3. Define the strongest defensible difference in the buyer's language.
4. Document where the product wins, where it loses, and why.
5. Draft concise response patterns for the most common objections.
6. Update the battlecard or competitive note so sales can use it immediately.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Honest comparison over self-flattering claims
- Buyer-relevant differences over internal feature gaps
- Clear win scenarios and clear loss scenarios
- Short responses that sales can remember and repeat
- Claims backed by evidence, not just opinion

## Output Contract

- Competitive positioning summary
- Battlecard or objection response set
- Win/loss notes and the strongest differentiating proof point
- Any caveats about where the competitor is stronger

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Competitor: Established incumbent with broad platform coverage
- Need: Help sales respond to "why not just use them?"

Expected output:
- Response: "If you need a broad suite, they're credible. If you need the fastest path to this specific outcome, we win because the workflow is purpose-built and easier to adopt."

## Guardrails

- Do not pretend the competitor has no strengths
- Do not collapse differentiation into a feature checklist
- Do not use claims that the buyer cannot verify
- Do not confuse a rebuttal with a complete competitive strategy
