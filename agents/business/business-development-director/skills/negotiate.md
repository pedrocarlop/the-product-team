# Negotiate

## Purpose

Use this skill to enter a strategic negotiation prepared, aligned, and clear about what can be traded, what cannot, and when to walk away.

## When to Use

- When a deal is moving from structure into active term discussion
- When the negotiation has meaningful economic, strategic, or reputational stakes
- When internal teams need a shared position before the conversation begins

## When Not to Use

- When the task is still to decide whether the opportunity is worth pursuing
- When the work is only target sourcing or research
- When the deal shape is still too undefined to negotiate usefully

## Required Inputs

- The current structure or term concept
- The company’s objectives and non-negotiables
- The counterparty’s likely priorities and alternatives
- Any internal dependencies, approvals, or constraints
- The deadline, decision process, and next negotiation milestone

## Workflow

1. Write the BATNA for both sides and the zone of possible agreement.
2. Identify the terms that matter most and the order in which they can be traded.
3. Confirm internal alignment on non-standard commitments before discussion.
4. Prepare a concession sequence that protects the core value of the deal.
5. Run the conversation to surface tradeoffs, not just to defend a position.
6. Capture the outcome clearly so legal or leadership can convert it into documents.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Preparation over improvisation
- Clarity over bluffing
- Strategic patience over premature concession
- Mutual executable value over cosmetic victory
- Internal alignment before external commitment

## Output Contract

- A written BATNA and walk-away position
- A concession plan with priority order
- A summary of agreed and unresolved terms
- Any internal follow-up required before the next round

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Deal: Potential OEM agreement with limited exclusivity

Expected output:
- BATNA analysis
- Concession order for exclusivity, pricing, and term length
- Summary of the terms that still need approval

## Guardrails

- Do not negotiate without a clear walk-away position
- Do not promise internal work that has not been confirmed
- Do not trade away core economics too early
- Do not leave the conversation without a written recap of open items
