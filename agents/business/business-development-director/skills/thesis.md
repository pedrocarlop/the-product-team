# Thesis

## Purpose

Use this skill to decide whether a business development opportunity is worth pursuing and to state, in writing, why it creates non-linear value for the company.

## When to Use

- When assessing a new market, strategic account, JV, acquisition target, or ecosystem play
- When leadership needs a clear investment thesis before resources are spent
- When the opportunity is structurally different from the standard sales motion

## When Not to Use

- When the question is only how to find prospects or counterparties
- When the deal structure is already known and the task is to draft terms
- When the work is mainly negotiation prep or concession sequencing

## Required Inputs

- The opportunity being considered, including market, counterparty, or deal type
- The business goal the opportunity is meant to serve
- Available internal constraints such as product, legal, finance, and engineering capacity
- Any known competitive, regulatory, or timing factors
- Evidence already collected, even if incomplete

## Workflow

1. State the opportunity in one sentence, including the value being pursued.
2. Identify the specific reason the opportunity is not reachable through the standard sales motion.
3. Define the value creation logic: why this creates leverage, speed, access, margin, or defensibility.
4. Test the thesis against the company’s constraints and current strategic priorities.
5. List the main assumptions, the earliest disconfirming signal, and the decision deadline.
6. Rewrite the thesis so an executive can approve, reject, or redirect it quickly.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Clear strategic logic over enthusiasm
- Specificity over market-size theater
- Evidence-backed assumptions over vague optimism
- Decision usefulness over narrative polish
- Alignment with company capabilities and timing

## Output Contract

- A concise written thesis
- The supporting assumptions and evidence gaps
- The disconfirming signal to watch for
- A clear recommendation to pursue, pause, or drop the opportunity

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Opportunity: A regional software vendor wants an OEM-style partnership
- Goal: Open a new distribution channel

Expected output:
- Thesis: "This partnership is worth pursuing if it gives us access to a buyer segment we cannot reach efficiently through direct sales and if the vendor can influence purchase decisions at scale."
- Rationale: States why the opportunity matters and what must be true for it to create value.

## Guardrails

- Do not confuse a large market with a good thesis
- Do not write a thesis that depends on unconfirmed future product work
- Do not approve opportunities that lack a believable path to execution
- Do not skip the failure case or disconfirming signal
