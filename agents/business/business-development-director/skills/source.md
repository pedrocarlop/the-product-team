# Source

## Purpose

Use this skill to build a high-quality list of targets and evidence: who matters, why they matter, and what signals suggest they are worth approaching.

## When to Use

- When starting a BD motion and needing target companies, partners, or acquirers
- When mapping an ecosystem, category, or market landscape
- When identifying executive stakeholders or relationship entry points

## When Not to Use

- When the target list already exists and the work is to define the thesis
- When the issue is contract structure or commercial terms
- When the user needs negotiation strategy or BATNA analysis

## Required Inputs

- The market, segment, or strategic problem to source around
- Filters such as geography, company size, industry, or use case
- Any known must-haves, deal breakers, or ideal customer characteristics
- The desired outcome: lead list, ecosystem map, stakeholder map, or intelligence brief

## Workflow

1. Clarify the sourcing objective and the kind of target being sought.
2. Identify the highest-signal sources of truth, including public web sources and internal records.
3. Build a candidate list with enough context to explain why each target belongs on it.
4. Capture decision-makers, influencers, and relationship entry points where available.
5. Score or rank the targets by strategic fit, urgency, access, and execution likelihood.
6. Remove weak leads and surface the strongest paths to a first conversation.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Signal density over sheer list length
- Strategic relevance over generic completeness
- Decision-maker visibility over surface-level company names
- Current evidence over stale assumptions
- Actionability over raw research volume

## Output Contract

- A ranked list of targets or sources
- Short notes explaining why each target matters
- Known stakeholders, contacts, or access paths
- Gaps that still need validation

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Market: Payment infrastructure partners in Southern Europe
- Goal: Find 10 likely strategic counterparties

Expected output:
- A ranked list of companies with notes on product fit, market reach, and likely executive contacts
- A summary of which targets look strongest and why

## Guardrails

- Do not treat an unqualified list as a finished sourcing deliverable
- Do not over-index on company size without strategic fit
- Do not confuse contact availability with real opportunity
- Do not ignore exclusion criteria or market timing

## Optional Tools / Resources

- Web search for company, market, and competitive research
- CRM or pipeline records for relationship history
- LinkedIn or similar executive profiles when available
- Internal notes on prior outreach, partnerships, and deal history
