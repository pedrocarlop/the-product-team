# Scan

## Purpose

Use this skill to gather the outside-in evidence that shapes strategy: market structure, customer behavior, competitor positioning, and emerging signals that could change the decision.

## When to Use

- When you need current market, category, or competitor intelligence
- When the team needs to understand where the market is going and why
- When you are validating whether an opportunity is real, growing, or defensible
- When executive decisions depend on external facts that may have changed recently

## When Not to Use

- When the question is already answered and the team needs a decision or plan
- When the task is mainly comparing in-house options or building an operating plan
- When the answer only requires internal data or historical reporting

## Required Inputs

- The strategic question and why external evidence matters
- The category, segment, geography, or competitor set in scope
- The time horizon that matters for the decision
- Any known sources, hypotheses, or prior research to test
- Constraints on depth, recency, and citation quality

## Workflow

1. Define the market or competitor universe that matters for the decision.
2. Gather recent, reliable sources and separate facts from interpretation.
3. Identify structural forces: size, growth, buyer behavior, pricing, margins, regulation, or technology shifts.
4. Map direct, adjacent, and potential competitors or substitutes.
5. Surface the strongest signals, the weak signals, and the noise to ignore.
6. Translate the scan into strategic implications, not just observations.
7. Note what still needs validation before recommendations are made.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Recency and source quality matter
- Facts should be traceable to credible sources
- Structure before volume
- External evidence should lead to strategic implications
- Signal, trend, and anomaly should be distinguished clearly

## Output Contract

- Market and competitor scan with sources
- Key trends and structural forces
- Strategic implications and risk points
- Open questions that still need validation

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Request: "Is this market attractive enough to pursue?"

Expected output:
- Scan: market size ranges, growth drivers, buyer economics, key competitors, and emergent threats
- Implication: whether the opportunity is expanding, crowded, or structurally unattractive
- Validation gaps: assumptions that still need customer or internal confirmation

## Guardrails

- Do not rely on a single source or a single opinion
- Do not confuse feature lists with strategic insight
- Do not present stale market facts as if they are current
- Do not turn the scan into a recommendation without moving through comparison and planning

## Optional Tools / Resources

- Web search and primary source research
- Internal customer and sales notes
- Pricing pages, earnings calls, and product announcements
- Market reports, analyst notes, and regulatory updates
