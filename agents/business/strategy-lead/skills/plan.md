# Plan

## Purpose

Use this skill to convert a strategic direction into an executable plan that the organization can own, schedule, and measure.

## When to Use

- When leadership needs a strategic plan, operating plan, or planning calendar
- When the company needs OKRs that reflect the strategy rather than activities
- When the team must sequence initiatives across functions and time horizons
- When a board or executive process needs a durable written output

## When Not to Use

- When the strategy question is still unclear
- When the comparison of options has not yet been completed
- When the team only needs a market scan or a decision memo

## Required Inputs

- The approved strategy or recommendation
- The planning horizon and audience
- Resource constraints, dependencies, and non-negotiables
- The outcomes the plan must change
- Any existing annual or quarterly commitments that constrain sequencing

## Workflow

1. Restate the strategic choice and the outcomes the plan must produce.
2. Break the plan into workstreams, milestones, and owners.
3. Sequence work by dependency, risk, and time to signal.
4. Define measurable outcomes and OKRs that reflect the strategy.
5. Identify resourcing, cross-functional coordination, and decision points.
6. Document risks, assumptions, and triggers for course correction.
7. Package the plan so it can be executed and reviewed without the author present.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Execution should trace back to the strategy
- The plan should be specific enough to assign ownership
- OKRs should measure outcomes, not just activity
- Sequencing should respect dependencies and capacity
- The written plan should support alignment across functions and leadership

## Output Contract

- Strategic or operating plan with milestones, owners, and sequencing
- OKRs or success measures tied to the strategy
- Key risks, dependencies, and decision points
- Planning calendar or review cadence when relevant

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Request: "Turn the enterprise growth recommendation into next quarter's plan."

Expected output:
- Plan: workstreams, owners, milestones, and weekly or monthly checkpoints
- OKRs: outcome-based metrics that show whether the strategy is working
- Risks: the assumptions that need active monitoring

## Guardrails

- Do not create a plan before the strategic choice is clear
- Do not confuse activity lists with an executable strategy
- Do not set OKRs that are just output counting
- Do not omit the decision points that determine whether the plan should change

- Strategy memos and decision logs
