# Discovery

## Purpose

Use this skill to uncover the technical and organizational facts that determine whether the product can work in the prospect's environment and what evidence will matter most.

## When to Use

- When a prospect first raises a technical need or evaluation question
- When the environment, integrations, identity, data, or security constraints are not yet clear
- When you need to qualify whether a demo, PoC, or security review is the right next step
- When the technical buyer, champion, and approval process need to be mapped

## When Not to Use

- When the requirements are already clear and the work is to build or present a demo
- When the task is to prove a single technical claim with evidence or a prototype
- When the main work is managing a known objection or risk with a tailored response

## Required Inputs

- Prospect name, deal stage, and primary business problem
- Known technical stack, including identity, data, integration, and hosting constraints
- The technical stakeholders involved and who will approve the evaluation
- The specific question the prospect needs answered to move forward
- Any prior notes, call transcripts, security questionnaires, or RFP context

## Workflow

1. Identify the exact technical decision that discovery needs to support.
2. Map the prospect's environment: systems, identity provider, data flows, deployment model, and security process.
3. Separate confirmed facts from assumptions and unresolved questions.
4. Clarify who will evaluate the solution and what each stakeholder needs to see.
5. Translate the discovery findings into a demo, PoC, or proof plan only after the requirements are explicit.
6. Document open risks, missing inputs, and required follow-up in a form the rest of the team can reuse.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Discovery should reduce ambiguity, not produce a generic checklist
- Facts from the prospect beat internal assumptions
- The evaluation path should match the buyer's technical reality
- Open questions should be explicit and owned
- Discovery output should make the next step obvious

## Output Contract

- A structured discovery summary
- The prospect's environment, constraints, and stakeholders
- Open questions and risks that still need answers
- A recommended next step, such as demo, proof, or de-risking work

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Prospect: Enterprise SaaS buyer
- Need: "Can this work with Okta, Snowflake, and our private cloud setup?"

Expected output:
- Summary: Identity uses Okta, data lives in Snowflake, and deployment constraints need validation before a demo
- Next step: Collect integration and security requirements, then design a targeted demo around those systems

## Guardrails

- Do not assume the prospect's stack is standard
- Do not jump to a demo before the technical question is clear
- Do not treat discovery as a generic qualification script
- Do not record commitments that have not been validated
