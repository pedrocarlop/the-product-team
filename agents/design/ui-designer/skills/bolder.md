# Bolder

Use this skill to raise the energy and memorability of a surface that feels too cautious or interchangeable.

## When to Use

- When the design feels generic, timid, or visually forgettable
- When the hierarchy is technically correct but lacks presence
- When the product needs more character without breaking usability

## How to Use

Choose one or two levers to amplify intentionally: display typography, contrast, color emphasis, composition, or a signature visual motif. Strengthen the focal point and let supporting content recede more clearly. Make the experience feel chosen, not defaulted.

Avoid substituting effects for design judgment. More gradients, more blur, or more animation does not equal stronger design. The direction should still read as coherent if the decorative layer is stripped away.

## What to Produce

- A sharper visual hierarchy with a clearly dominant focal point
- The specific visual moves that add energy or character
- Any token, component, or layout updates needed to support the stronger direction
- A short note on what was intentionally left restrained

## Notes for UI Designer

Bold work is still disciplined work. If every element becomes louder, nothing becomes memorable.
