# Delight

Use this skill to improve the emotional quality of an experience that works functionally but still feels flat or joyless.

## When to Use

- When a feature needs more personality, warmth, or reward in key moments
- When loading, success, or empty states feel purely mechanical
- When the product would benefit from small memorable moments that reinforce trust or satisfaction

## How to Use

Map the emotional arc of the experience first. Identify moments of anticipation, uncertainty, recovery, and success, then choose where a small layer of delight would help users feel guided, relieved, or rewarded. Favor low-friction interventions such as celebration, supportive feedback, richer empty states, or a more human interaction tone.

Keep the additions proportional to the product and the stakes of the task. Delight should strengthen the experience users are already trying to complete, not interrupt it or call attention to itself more than the work.

## What to Produce

- A prioritized set of delight opportunities tied to specific moments in the flow
- The intended user effect for each moment and why it belongs there
- Any cross-role handoff needed for UI, motion, or content execution

## Notes for Product Designer

Good delight compounds over time. If the idea would feel embarrassing in a serious context or exhausting when repeated, it is not the right kind of delight.
