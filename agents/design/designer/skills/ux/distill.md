# Distill

Use this skill to simplify a feature, screen, or flow that asks users to process too much at once.

## When to Use

- When a task flow has too many decisions, branches, or parallel concepts
- When a screen feels cluttered because too much structure is visible at once
- When the design needs simplification before visual refinement can succeed

## How to Use

Identify the primary task, the minimum information needed to complete it, and the decisions that genuinely change the outcome. Remove or defer anything that does not help the user move forward. Use progressive disclosure, better grouping, and cleaner sequencing to make complexity appear only when it becomes relevant.

Simplify at the flow and information-architecture level first. If the surface still feels complex after the sequence is clear, then hand the remaining visual cleanup to UI-focused refinement.

## What to Produce

- A leaner task structure with fewer exposed decisions
- Simplified screen or flow logic, including anything deferred or removed
- Clear rationale for what was kept visible versus disclosed later

## Notes for UX Designer

Distillation is not stripping the product down blindly. Keep the complexity users actually need, and remove the complexity they should not have to carry.
