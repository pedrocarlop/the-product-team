# Audit

Use this skill to evaluate the technical quality of a designed or implemented interface without stepping into full redesign.

## When to Use

- When a surface needs a structured quality review before release
- When accessibility, responsiveness, theming, or performance risks need to be documented
- When the team needs clear remediation priorities grounded in evidence

## How to Use

Review the interface across five dimensions: accessibility, performance, responsive behavior, theming or token discipline, and implementation anti-patterns. Use measurable evidence where possible, then assign severity based on user impact and release risk. Keep taste-based critique out of this skill unless it reveals a technical anti-pattern that affects quality directly.

Score the overall health of the surface, but do not stop at scoring. The point of the audit is to make the next remediation pass obvious and actionable.

## What to Produce

- A prioritized audit report with findings grouped by severity
- Evidence for each issue: impacted surface, user impact, and standard or constraint involved
- A short health summary across the core dimensions reviewed
- Clear remediation guidance for the owning design or engineering role

## Notes for Design Technologist

This skill is for technical quality, not general design taste. Route flow, copy, and heuristic issues to the dedicated design reviewers when they are outside the technical layer.
