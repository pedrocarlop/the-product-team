# Systematize

## Purpose

Use this skill to turn repeated design operations work into a clear, repeatable system that people can follow without having to ask every time.

## When to Use

- When the same operational task keeps being re-explained or rebuilt
- When a workflow needs an intake path, checklist, template, or approval sequence
- When handoffs are slow because ownership, inputs, or outputs are unclear

## When Not to Use

- When the task is primarily about setting standards or enforcing policy
- When the work is a scheduling problem rather than a process problem
- When the issue is a single exception and not a recurring pattern

## Required Inputs

- The recurring task or workflow that needs structure
- The people or roles involved in the workflow
- The inputs, outputs, and decision points in the current process
- Any known friction, delays, or failure modes
- Constraints such as tooling, compliance, or required approvals

## Workflow

1. Map the current process from first trigger to final handoff.
2. Identify the steps that are repeated, inconsistent, or easy to miss.
3. Define the standard path, including owners, inputs, outputs, and checkpoints.
4. Add the minimum templates, checklists, or forms needed to make the process usable.
5. Pilot the process with a real case and adjust for friction or ambiguity.
6. Document the final version so the team can use it without recreating the logic.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Reduce variance without adding unnecessary ceremony
- Make ownership and decision points explicit
- Prefer reusable templates over repeated explanation
- Keep the process easy to find, follow, and maintain
- Design for the next person who has to run the workflow

## Output Contract

- A documented workflow or SOP
- Required templates, checklists, or intake forms
- Ownership and approval map
- Known exceptions and how they should be handled
- Notes from the pilot or validation pass

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Problem: Design requests arrive through Slack, email, and ad hoc messages
- Need: A consistent way to intake and prioritize requests

Expected output:
- Intake flow: Single request form with required fields
- Checklist: Triage steps for urgency, scope, and owner assignment
- Handoff note: Where requests go after intake and who approves exceptions

## Guardrails

- Do not create process steps that nobody owns
- Do not add checkpoints that do not change the outcome
- Do not turn a simple workflow into a bureaucracy
- Do not leave the team with a process that only exists in the author's head

## Optional Tools / Resources

- Notion for SOPs and playbooks
- Figma for annotated workflow diagrams
- Spreadsheets or forms for intake and tracking
- Existing process docs, retro notes, or support examples
