# Schedule

## Purpose

Use this skill to design a workable cadence for design operations so the right people are in the right room at the right time without creating calendar chaos.

## When to Use

- When a recurring review, ritual, or checkpoint needs a stable schedule
- When multiple deadlines or launches need to be sequenced against team capacity
- When meetings are multiplying and no one can tell which ones are essential

## When Not to Use

- When the main issue is unclear ownership or process structure
- When the problem is policy, standards, or escalation rather than timing
- When a one-time meeting invite is all that is needed

## Required Inputs

- The recurring activity or milestone that needs scheduling
- The people, roles, and time zones involved
- Any fixed dates, dependencies, or blackout periods
- Capacity limits, review windows, or expected prep time
- The desired outcome of each meeting or cadence

## Workflow

1. List every recurring event, milestone, or checkpoint that affects the work.
2. Separate fixed commitments from flexible cadences and decide what can move.
3. Sequence the schedule around dependencies, time zones, and capacity constraints.
4. Assign owners, attendees, and prep requirements for each event.
5. Check the cadence for overload, redundancy, or missed decision points.
6. Publish the schedule in a form the team can actually follow and maintain.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Schedule around decisions, not just availability
- Protect focus time and avoid unnecessary meetings
- Make cadence predictable enough to build habits
- Align prep effort with the value of the decision being made
- Keep the calendar understandable for people joining later

## Output Contract

- A cadence or calendar plan
- Meeting purpose, owner, and attendee map
- Key dates, milestones, and dependencies
- Capacity or coverage notes
- Exception handling for skipped or shifted events

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Need: Set a weekly design ops review
- Constraint: Participants are split across three time zones

Expected output:
- Cadence: Weekly review at a time that overlaps all core attendees
- Meeting rule: Only agenda items that need a decision or unblock
- Coverage note: Rotate note-taking or follow-up ownership if a stakeholder cannot attend

## Guardrails

- Do not schedule meetings without a clear decision purpose
- Do not overload the same people with too many recurring checkpoints
- Do not create a cadence that ignores time zones or prep burden
- Do not let the calendar become the process

## Optional Tools / Resources

- Calendar and scheduling tools
- Capacity trackers or team planning sheets
- Milestone plans, launch calendars, and review queues
- Ownership docs or attendance records
