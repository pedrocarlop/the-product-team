# Interview

## Purpose

Use this skill to conduct interviews that surface user behavior, mental models, and decision-making without leading participants toward the answer we want.

## When to Use

- When the question is about why people behave a certain way
- When you need context, motivations, workarounds, or vocabulary from the user
- When a session requires a discussion guide and a clean note-taking structure

## When Not to Use

- When the goal is to measure task success or compare interface performance
- When the main work is study planning or synthesis
- When the user already knows the answer and only needs a concise summary

## Required Inputs

- The research question and decision the interview should inform
- Participant profile and why those participants matter
- The topics or behaviors to explore
- Any known risks, sensitive topics, or constraints on the session
- The note-taking format or output expected after the interview

## Workflow

1. Write the interview objective in plain language.
2. Create a guide that starts broad and moves toward specifics.
3. Use open prompts, follow-ups, and examples instead of leading questions.
4. Stay close to real behavior, recent experiences, and concrete moments.
5. Capture notable quotes, contradictions, workarounds, and hesitation points.
6. Debrief immediately after the session while observations are still fresh.
7. Separate what the participant said from what the researcher inferred.

## Design Principles / Evaluation Criteria

- Questions invite stories, not rehearsed opinions
- The guide supports flow, not a rigid script
- Observations are grounded in what was actually said or shown
- Debriefs happen immediately so nuance is not lost
- The final notes distinguish evidence from interpretation

## Output Contract

- Interview objective and participant context
- Discussion guide or topic outline
- Session notes with quotes, behaviors, and notable themes
- Immediate debrief with early implications or follow-up questions

## Examples

### Example 1

Input:
- Topic: why users abandon a setup flow
- Participant: recent signups who did not finish onboarding

Expected output:
- A discussion guide focused on their last attempt, what they expected, where they hesitated, and what they tried next
- Notes that separate direct quotes from the researcher’s interpretation

## Guardrails

- Do not ask users what they want as a substitute for observing behavior
- Do not explain the proposed solution during the interview
- Do not treat a single interview as proof of a pattern
- Do not skip the debrief after the session

## Optional Tools / Resources

- Zoom or Google Meet for the call
- Transcription support if available
- Notion for notes, debriefs, and interview summaries
