# Critique

Use this skill to give a direct, structured usability critique of a design, screen, feature, or component set.

## When to Use

- When a design needs holistic feedback before refinement or approval
- When the team wants a heuristic review that goes beyond surface-level opinion
- When it is important to identify the few issues most likely to hurt task success

## How to Use

Evaluate the design across the dimensions that most affect usability: visual hierarchy, information architecture, cognitive load, emotional journey, discoverability, affordance, typography, color usage, state coverage, and copy clarity. Start by checking for generic or low-intent design tells that suggest the surface is visually derivative rather than thoughtfully composed.

Score the interface against Nielsen's heuristics, identify the most relevant user modes or personas for the task, and focus the write-up on the issues that most directly harm understanding, confidence, or completion. Keep praise specific and criticism actionable.

## What to Evaluate

- A usability health score grounded in heuristic review
- The three to five highest-value issues, each with severity, impact, and concrete fix direction
- Relevant persona or user-mode red flags such as first-time use, expert efficiency, or high-anxiety recovery moments
- The strongest positive patterns worth preserving

## Notes for Usability Reviewer

Do not soften the findings into generic encouragement. A good critique is specific enough that the owning designer knows exactly what to fix next without mistaking this for a full redesign brief.
