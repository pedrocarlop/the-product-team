# Overdrive

Use this skill when the goal is not just to refine a surface, but to create an extraordinary interaction or presentation moment that still respects product context and technical reality.

## When to Use

- When the user explicitly wants a high-ambition, standout, or "wow" interaction
- When a feature could benefit from advanced motion, rendering, or responsiveness techniques
- When the team is willing to trade implementation complexity for a noticeably stronger experience

## How to Use

Define what "extraordinary" should mean for this product and moment first. Then frame two or three viable directions with different ambition levels, technical approaches, and tradeoffs. Choose the direction that best fits the brand, task, and performance budget before pushing into detailed design or prototype work.

Build the concept with discipline: progressive enhancement, reduced-motion behavior, fallback rendering, and real-world performance guardrails. Extraordinary work still has to be usable on normal devices.

## What to Produce

- A short set of concept directions with tradeoffs and risks
- The selected direction and the reason it fits this context best
- A feasibility brief covering enhancement strategy, fallbacks, and verification criteria

## Notes for Design Technologist

Technical ambition is only impressive when it serves the experience. If the effect overshadows the task or fails on mid-range devices, it is not ready.
