# Onboard

Use this skill to improve how new or returning users learn the product and reach their first meaningful success.

## When to Use

- When a feature needs first-run guidance, activation support, or empty-state teaching
- When users are dropping off before reaching the product's "aha" moment
- When the current onboarding explains too much while delivering too little value

## How to Use

Define the target user, their starting knowledge, the shortest path to value, and the first outcome that proves the product is useful. Decide what must be taught up front, what can be revealed in context, and what experienced users should be allowed to skip.

Design onboarding around real product actions, not ceremony. Use empty states, progressive disclosure, and contextual prompts to teach the next useful thing at the moment it matters.

## What to Produce

- An onboarding sequence or first-run flow tied to the key activation moment
- Clear empty-state and contextual-guidance patterns for the surface
- Success criteria for knowing whether the onboarding experience worked

## Notes for UX Designer

The goal is not to explain the whole product. The goal is to reduce anxiety, get users moving, and help them succeed fast enough to want the second step.
