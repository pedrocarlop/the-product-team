## New design check
This is a new design for a greenfield SaaS project management tool.

## Reference selection
1. Linear - For its minimalist and craft-heavy execution.
2. Asana - For its approachable and colorful task management.
3. Notion - For its flexible, content-first layout.

## Direction 1: Minimalist Precision
- **Visual thesis**: A clean, high-density interface focused on power users.
- **Style pillars**: Sharp corners, thin borders, monochrome palette.
- **Token direction**: Grays and deep blacks. Secondary accent: #007AFF.
- **Reference cues**: Linear.
- **Divergence axes**: Density vs Spacing, Light vs Dark.
- **Why this is materially different**: Low visual noise, high utility.

## Direction 2: Playful Productivity
- **Visual thesis**: An approachable, card-based UI with rounded corners.
- **Style pillars**: 8px border radius, soft shadows, vibrant accents.
- **Token direction**: Primary: #6200EE. Success: #03DAC6.
- **Reference cues**: Asana.
- **Divergence axes**: Roundedness, Color Saturation.
- **Why this is materially different**: Focuses on emotional engagement.

## Direction 3: Canvas-First
- **Visual thesis**: A flexible, blocks-based layout.
- **Style pillars**: Drag-and-drop handles, spacious margins.
- **Token direction**: Neutral backgrounds with blue links.
- **Reference cues**: Notion.
- **Divergence axes**: Layout model.
- **Why this is materially different**: Prioritizes content flexibility over fixed lists.

## Project ds-spec seed
- **Typography**: Arial, sans-serif.
- **Color Palette**: 
  - Primary: #007AFF
  - Background: #FFFFFF
  - Text: #333333
- **Spacing**: 4px, 8px, 16px, 32px increments.

## Recommended direction
Direction 1: Minimalist Precision. This fits the "SaaS project management" power-user persona best.

## Reflection
The directions are distinct but could be more visually daring in terms of modern trends.
